The DeFi landscape, end-to-end:
how the money moved, 2017 → 2026.

Decentralized finance went from a ~$0.6B science project at the end of 2019 to a $177.5B all-time high in November 2021, survived the Terra/FTX implosion that erased ~78% of it, re-approached its peak at ~$171B in October 2025, and sits at ~$74.5B today after a sharp 2026 drawdown. Beneath the headline TVL, the composition changed completely: liquid staking, restaking, perps, stablecoins and real-world assets reshaped what DeFi actually is. This page assembles the hard numbers, the cycle story, the protocol map, and what the last 12 months of conferences were actually about.

Headline series follow DefiLlama's consistent cross-chain methodology; other trackers cite higher peaks (e.g. ~$202–237B) under broader inclusion rules. Every chart links to its underlying raw file. See sources & methodology.

The headline that matters: dollar TVL re-approached its 2021 high in 2025, but the mix inverted. In 2020–21 DEXs, lending and MakerDAO dominated. This page was compiled from a multi-agent deep-research run (98 agents, ~2.7M tokens) that fanned out across the open web, extracted falsifiable claims with source URLs and as-of dates, and adversarially fact-checked the load-bearing numbers (3-vote verification). Read these caveats before quoting a number

  • Methodology divergence. The headline TVL series uses DefiLlama's consistent cross-chain methodology (ATH ~$177.5B). Other trackers cite ~$202–237B for the 2021/2025 peaks under broader inclusion/double-counting rules.
  • Gross category sums double-count. Per-category totals (e.g. Bridge ~$46.7B, restaking + LRTs) include wrapped/restaked assets that count the same underlying ETH/BTC more than once. Treat them as upper bounds.
  • TVL is price-driven. Much of the 2026 USD drawdown reflects falling ETH/SOL prices, not mass withdrawals — staked-ETH counts kept rising even as USD TVL fell.
  • Addresses ≠ people. Cumulative-address user counts (~333M) massively overstate real users (~40–70M monthly active worldwide).
  • Volume figures vary by source/scope (spot-only vs. incl. perps; CoinGecko vs. DefiLlama). Ranges are given where sources disagree.
  • Conference detail confidence varies — future-dated 2026 events and events with thin public agendas are lower-confidence; speakers/panels are never fabricated, and adjacent-but-out-of-window events are flagged.
